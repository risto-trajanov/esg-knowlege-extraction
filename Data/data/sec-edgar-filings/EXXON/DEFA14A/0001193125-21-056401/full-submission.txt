 </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>How ExxonMobil Is Reducing Greenhouse Gas Emissions: Our 2025 Emission-Reduction Plans </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Before 2020 ended, ExxonMobil announced plans to reduce greenhouse gas emissions from its operations over the next five years. As 2021 unfolds, those plans
will be at the forefront of our minds, guiding our strategy and operations. The following are a few highlights outlining our emission-reduction plans:</P> <P STYLE="font-size:6pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>
<TABLE STYLE="BORDER-COLLAPSE:COLLAPSE; font-family:Times New Roman; font-size:10pt" BORDER="0" CELLPADDING="0" CELLSPACING="0" WIDTH="100%">

<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"> <P ALIGN="left" STYLE=" margin-top:0pt ; margin-bottom:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman; font-size:10pt">We plan to reduce Upstream GHG emissions intensity by <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">15-20%</FONT> compared to
2016 levels. </P></TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="font-size:6pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>
<TABLE STYLE="BORDER-COLLAPSE:COLLAPSE; font-family:Times New Roman; font-size:10pt" BORDER="0" CELLPADDING="0" CELLSPACING="0" WIDTH="100%">
<TR style = "page-break-inside:avoid">

<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"> <P ALIGN="left" STYLE=" margin-top:0pt ; margin-bottom:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman; font-size:10pt">We plan to reduce methane emissions intensity by <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">40-50%</FONT> and decrease
flaring intensity across our global operations by <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">35-45%</FONT> compared to 2016 levels. </P></TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">These plans
cover Scope 1 and 2 emissions from assets operated by the company and are expected to reduce absolute greenhouse gas emissions by an estimated 30% across the company&#146;s Upstream business.&nbsp;We&#146;ve also started providing Scope 3 emissions
earlier this month in our Energy&nbsp;&amp; Carbon Summary, which we&#146;ll continue to do on an annual basis. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Our 2025 emission-reduction plans, which
were developed as part of our planning and budgeting process, are designed to be credible and tangible. Looking beyond 2025, I am hopeful that our company will be well poised to bring forth new scalable solutions to further mitigate GHG emissions
while providing affordable, reliable energy that upholds economies and living standards. </P>

<p style="margin-top:1em; margin-bottom:0em; page-break-before:always">
<HR SIZE="3" style="COLOR:#999999" WIDTH="100%" ALIGN="CENTER">


<Center><DIV STYLE="width:8.5in" align="left">
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>ExxonMobil EnergyFactor.com Content </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In the short term, the 2025 reduction plans we&#146;ve outlined are projected to be consistent with the trajectories of a
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">2-degree</FONT> pathway, a vision articulated in the Paris Agreement that translates to about a 10% reduction in global GHG emissions by 2025 versus 2016 numbers. Solving the dual challenge requires all of us to be
in this together, and companies like ours can bring unique strengths and scale that help put societies on a path to a lower-carbon tomorrow. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">How are we
expecting to achieve these plans? </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">A significant portion of this reduction will come from our unconventional Upstream production in the U.S., where we are
continuing to reduce flaring and methane emissions. To this end, in collaboration with others in the space, we have developed a series of industry-leading best practices, including increasing leak detection and repair, improving inspections with
advanced technologies, and retiring high-bleed pneumatic devices (nearly 100% of these devices were phased out from our U.S. unconventional operations as of 2020). We&#146;ve also offered a <U>methane regulatory framework</U> to encourage
governments to enact similar requirements across the industry while working with universities to advance research and monitoring networks.</P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Similarly, our
Downstream and Chemical operations will continue to focus on improving energy efficiency, increasing cogeneration at manufacturing facilities, and applying new technologies and techniques that reduce emissions. One example I wrote about recently is
the redesigned hydrocracking system we deployed in Rotterdam that won an Edison Award from the R&amp;D Council of New Jersey. In the past few years, my team has developed novel materials, liquid separation membranes, and <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">gas-treating</FONT> technologies that can achieve significant greenhouse gas reductions. A case in point is<B>&nbsp;Celestia</B><SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&#153;</SUP>, a breakthrough catalyst that can
help refiners efficiently remove sulfur and other impurities from diesel, an essential fuel for commercial transportation, while using less energy and creating fewer emissions.</P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Our efforts to reduce emissions in the short term run parallel to our commitment to finding solutions in the long term. Today, our eyes are focused on the
technology advancements that will be crucial to achieving society&#146;s <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">net-zero</FONT> ambitions. These include carbon capture and storage, <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">low-carbon</FONT> hydrogen production,
advanced biofuels and energy-efficient manufacturing. Importantly, these research directions represent technologies that apply to three sectors of energy use (commercial transportation, power generation and industrial manufacturing) that currently
have only limited viable solutions for reducing emissions. For instance, CCS, a technology that the IPCC has identified as necessary in every projected <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">2-degrees</FONT> pathway, can be deployed at power and
industrial facilities. On that note, ExxonMobil has captured more CO2 than any other company since the inception of the technology. And with our ongoing collaborations with companies like Global Thermostat and Fuel Cell, as well as more than 80
universities around the world, we are uniquely positioned to bring this portfolio of technologies to greater scale. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Like I&#146;ve said before, solving
the dual challenge will require conviction, ingenuity and, most importantly, collaboration. The plans for 2025 we&#146;ve outlined, coupled with insights from our recent Energy&nbsp;&amp; Carbon Summary, represent our progress&nbsp;and vision.
