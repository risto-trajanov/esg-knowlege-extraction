<IMG SRC="g168614g0408182456731.jpg" ALT="LOGO">
 </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Bridger Photonics&#146; high-tech laser solution selected by ExxonMobil for EPA Methane Detection </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Bozeman, MT, April</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;8, 2021 &#151;</B> <U>Bridger Photonics</U>&#146; Light Detection and Ranging (<U>LiDAR</U>) technology was selected
by ExxonMobil for incorporation into the first-ever filing of an application with the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (<U>EPA</U>) to use cutting-edge technology for methane emissions and other regulatory compliance. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">EPA Regulations (<U>40 CFR Part 60 Subpart OOOOa</U>) require oil and gas producers to inspect their equipment for leaks of methane volatile organic compounds
and other pollutants. The regulation prescribes that field crews must visit hundreds of thousands of U.S. production sites on foot and inspect each piece of equipment by hand in search of leaks. While this leak detection process was considered the
best available at the time, it is costly and time consuming, and has increased field crews&#146; exposures to <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">on-site</FONT> hazards. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Bridger Photonics was selected after an extensive vetting process including <U>field trials</U> of emerging methane detection technologies to identify more
efficient, and more effective ways to detect and quantify methane leaks. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Instead of visiting sites on foot, Bridger Photonics scans sites from aircraft
using its advanced LiDAR technology, <U>Gas Mapping LiDAR</U><SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&#153;</SUP>, to sensitively and quickly detect and estimate methane emissions throughout the entire natural gas value chain (production,
transmission, and distribution of natural gas). Bridger hands its clients a map that pinpoints (GPS coordinates), images, and estimates the quantity of every detectable emission in the client&#146;s infrastructure. These data products provide
actionable information for field crews to easily find and prioritize leaks. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;What used to take six months for field crews to inspect now takes a
matter of days with Gas Mapping LiDAR<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&#153;</SUP>,&#148; said Dr.&nbsp;Pete Roos, CEO and <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">co-founder</FONT> of Bridger Photonics. &#147;Bridger precisely locates and
accurately quantifies methane emissions across broad areas so operators only need to deploy field crews when a leak is detected. This is a <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">win-win</FONT> for everyone involved: Bridger enables responsible operations
while saving operators the cost and hazards of unnecessary visits to 60% to 90% of their sites.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;We look forward to the EPA process
establishing this alternative as equally effective as existing regulatory requirements,&#148; said Bart Cahir, senior vice president of unconventional at ExxonMobil. &#147;Our experience shows us the technology available today can detect leaks more
efficiently than the manual processes federal regulations now require. ExxonMobil is deploying next-generation detection technologies under real-world operating conditions as part of our overall commitment to reduce methane emissions in our
operations.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Bridger Photonics&#146; Gas Mapping LiDAR<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&#153;</SUP> is the first technology ever
included in, and ExxonMobil is the first company ever to file, an application for an Alternate Means of Emissions Limitation (AMEL) for the EPA regulation. The AMEL application aims to replace ground crew visits with Gas Mapping LiDAR<SUP
STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&#153;</SUP>. </P>
