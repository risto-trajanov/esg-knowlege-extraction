shareholders, too, have consistently registered their high approval of our Board&#146;s quality, with support of about 96% on average over the last 5 years. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:arial"><B>Composition and Succession Planning </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:arial">The Board has a well-established
succession planning process to ensure proactive and timely Board renewal. In this regard, the Board Affairs Committee has <I>Guidelines for the Selection of Non-Employee Directors</I>, which are published on ExxonMobil&#146;s website. At least
annually, these guidelines are reviewed to ensure that they reflect current needs. Diversity is a key consideration that figures prominently in the <I>Guidelines </I>and is reflected in the composition of the Board, which is comprised of leaders
from many different industries and backgrounds. The active refreshment and diversity afford a rich foundation for the Board to provide collective oversight of the Company&#146;s extensive and complex business. In seeking qualified candidates, the
Committee employs an executive search firm as well as considers referrals. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:arial">Over the last 5 years, there have been 5 non-executive director retirements, and 6 new
non-executive directors joining the Board. A total of eleven directors are standing for election in 2017, ten of which are independent. Board tenure of the nominated non-executive directors averages approximately 7 years. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:arial">The Board Affairs Committee also considers Board requirements and director skillsets in determining committee assignments. A substantial majority of the Board must meet
the independence standards described in the Board&#146;s <I>Corporate Governance Guidelines</I>, also published on ExxonMobil&#146;s website. All Board committee members meet applicable NYSE and SEC requirements. Importantly, for example, all
members of the Audit Committee are &#147;independent&#148; and &#147;financially literate&#148; under the NYSE standards, and the Chair and most other members of the Committee are &#147;audit committee financial experts&#148; under SEC rules. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:arial"><B>Compensation </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:arial">The compensation program that applies to the Board is intended
to remunerate directors on a competitive basis to ensure the attraction and retention of the best candidates. An external, independent consultant is employed to survey external practices each year to advise Board committees on director compensation.
Board members are also granted Company restricted stock under a shareholder-approved equity program. The program and its vesting requirements help to align the interests of directors with those of long-term shareholders and the long investment lead
times of the Company. The program has successfully attracted and retained top talent and we believe it supports the Company&#146;s long-term business focus. </P>
