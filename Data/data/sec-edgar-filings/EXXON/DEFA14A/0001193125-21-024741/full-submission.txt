<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>ExxonMobil Low Carbon Solutions to Commercialize Emission-Reduction Technology </B></P>

<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"> <P ALIGN="left" STYLE=" margin-top:0pt ; margin-bottom:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman; font-size:10pt">Initial focus on carbon capture and storage (CCS), a technology recognized as critical to achieving Paris
Agreement climate goals </P></TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="font-size:6pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>
<TABLE STYLE="BORDER-COLLAPSE:COLLAPSE; font-family:Times New Roman; font-size:10pt" BORDER="0" CELLPADDING="0" CELLSPACING="0" WIDTH="100%">

<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"> <P ALIGN="left" STYLE=" margin-top:0pt ; margin-bottom:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman; font-size:10pt">Advancing plans for over 20 new CCS opportunities to enable large-scale emission reductions
</P></TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">IRVING, Texas &#150; ExxonMobil said today it has created a new business to commercialize its extensive
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">low-carbon</FONT> technology portfolio. The new business, ExxonMobil Low Carbon Solutions, will initially focus on carbon capture and storage, one of the critical technologies required to achieve net zero emissions
and the climate goals outlined in the Paris Agreement. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">ExxonMobil Low Carbon Solutions is advancing plans for more than 20 new carbon capture and storage
opportunities around the world to enable large-scale emission reductions. ExxonMobil plans to invest $3&nbsp;billion on lower emission energy solutions through 2025. The business will be led by Joe Blommaert, who has more than 30 years of experience
in the industry with leadership roles in technology advancement, product marketing, and operations. The board of directors has elected him as a vice president of Exxon Mobil Corporation. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">CCS is the process of capturing CO2 that would otherwise be released into the atmosphere from industrial activity, and injecting it into deep geologic
formations for safe, secure and permanent storage. The United Nations Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change and the International Energy Agency agree that CCS is one of the most important <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">low-carbon</FONT>
technologies required to achieve societal climate goals at the lowest cost. CCS is also one of the only technologies that could enable some industry sectors to decarbonize, including the refining, chemicals, cement and steel sectors. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">ExxonMobil has more than 30 years of experience in CCS technology and was the first company to capture more than 120&nbsp;million tonnes of CO2, which is
equivalent to the emissions of more than 25&nbsp;million cars for one year. The company has an equity share in about <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">one-fifth</FONT> of global CO2 capture capacity and has captured approximately 40&nbsp;percent of
