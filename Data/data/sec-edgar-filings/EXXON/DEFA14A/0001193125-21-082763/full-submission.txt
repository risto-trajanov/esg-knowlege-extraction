<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>ExxonMobil Files Definitive Proxy Urging Shareholders to Support Director Candidates </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Today we issued our definitive proxy statement for this year&#146;s Annual Meeting of Shareholders, to be held on May 26. Through the proxy, we&#146;re asking
shareholders to support our board of director nominees as well as other proposals of importance to the company. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">This year, Engine No.&nbsp;1, a
newly-formed hedge fund with a very small ownership position in ExxonMobil, nominated four directors to oppose four of our director nominees. Engine No.&nbsp;1 is also urging the company to take other initiatives, including deep spending cuts beyond
those we&#146;ve carefully made to improve competitiveness, respond to the pandemic&#146;s impact and preserve the value of our industry-leading portfolio. While the company will continue to drive efficiencies and lower costs, Engine No. 1&#146;s
plan would jeopardize our ability to grow earnings and cash to fund the dividend and invest in future projects, including emission-reduction technologies to address the risks of climate change. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In short, their plan would put our long-term value and business strategy at risk. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Our board affairs committee met and considered the qualifications, skill sets and experience of all director candidates &#150; including those of Engine
No.&nbsp;1. The committee found that none of the Engine No.&nbsp;1 candidates has the global experience, leadership qualifications and skill sets required for a company of ExxonMobil&#146;s size and scope as we navigate the highly complex energy
transition. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">ExxonMobil&#146;s 12 director nominees, 11 of whom are independent, have experience leading some of the world&#146;s largest, most complex
and successful companies, and also possess a wide range of backgrounds, knowledge and skills relevant to our business and critical to the company&#146;s success. Three new independent directors were appointed earlier this year, further enhancing
expertise in capital allocation across industries, energy transition and environmental, social and governance (ESG) practices. They also bring additional experience helping companies navigate complex transitions while building value for
shareholders. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Over the next two months, ExxonMobil will be communicating with shareholders about the importance of supporting our director nominees.
Employees who own ExxonMobil shares will receive these communications, which will include important background information as well as instructions on how to vote, using the <B>BLUE </B>proxy card, to support ExxonMobil&#146;s board nominees. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Shareholder employees may also receive communications from Engine No.&nbsp;1, including a white proxy card. We urge you to vote using ExxonMobil&#146;s
<B>BLUE </B>proxy card and discard any white cards received. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">To learn more about our board of directors and how shareholders can vote at the annual
meeting, please visit <U>ww</U><U>w</U><U>.</U><U>x</U><U>om</U><U>d</U><U>ri</U><U>v</U><U>i</U><U>ng</U><U>v</U><U>a</U><U>lue</U><U>.</U><U>c</U><U>o</U><U>m</U>, where you can sign up for updates. </P>
