<Center><DIV STYLE="width:8.5in" align="left">
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><I>The following communication will appear in the Energy Factor Asia Pacific </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>ASIA PACIFIC ENERGY FACTOR ARTICLE: MAKING GREENER STEEL FOR HOMES OF THE FUTURE </B></P>
<P STYLE="font-size:18pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">


<IMG SRC="g103897g81h09.jpg" ALT="LOGO">
 </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">India is becoming a powerhouse in Asia, but how can this powerhouse meet its growing population&#146;s housing needs while
managing its carbon footprint? </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">A recent Asia Power Index, which ranks 26 countries by cultural, economic and military power across the region, placed
India as the fourth most powerful nation in the region, after China, the US and Japan. <SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">1</SUP> It also forecast that India will reach 40&nbsp;per cent of China&#146;s economic output by 2030.<SUP
STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">2</SUP> </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Along with economic power, India&#146;s population is slated to grow by a quarter, from
1.21&nbsp;billion in 2011 to about 1.52&nbsp;billion by 2036. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">According to India&#146;s Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, about 70% of this growth
will be in urban areas, with the country&#146;s urban population increasing from 377&nbsp;million in 2011 to 594&nbsp;million in 2036. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">To meet this
growing urban population, the country will need at least 25&nbsp;million new affordable houses by 2030.<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">3</SUP> And to build these new houses, India needs millions of tonnes of steel. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">India&#146;s steel demand, in part to support this massive housing need, is likely to quadruple over the next 30 years to almost 450&nbsp;million tonnes.<SUP
STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">4</SUP> </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">But steel-making is also the largest industrial source of carbon emissions in India, contributing to
high air pollution levels.<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">5</SUP> Additionally, India&#146;s iron and steel sector has the highest emissions footprint per unit of production
globally.<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">6</SUP> </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">If the steel industry stays on its current trajectory, its carbon emissions are expected
to leap from about 242&nbsp;million tonnes to almost 837&nbsp;million tonnes by 2050.<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">7</SUP> This can slow India down in meeting its <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">air-quality</FONT> goals and
challenge its cleaner-growth aspirations. </P> <P STYLE="font-size:6pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P> <P STYLE="font-size:6pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>
<P STYLE="font-size:6pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P> <P STYLE="font-size:6pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P> <P STYLE="font-size:6pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>
<P STYLE="font-size:6pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P> <P STYLE="line-height:8.0pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:2pt;border-bottom:1px solid #000000;width:11%">&nbsp;</P>
