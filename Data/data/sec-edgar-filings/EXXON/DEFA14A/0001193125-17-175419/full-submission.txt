<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">We would like to highlight several key messages to address comments raised by some shareholders regarding <I>Item&nbsp;12 &#150; Report on Impacts of Climate
Change Policies</I> in ExxonMobil&#146;s Proxy Statement that incorrectly characterize the company&#146;s position and actions. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Supporting the Paris
Agreement </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">ExxonMobil supports the Paris Agreement in our actions and communications. We advocate that the risks of climate change warrant thoughtful
action. As noted in our disclosures, including the new <I>Energy&nbsp;&amp; Carbon Summary (ECS)</I> which was distributed this year along with other Proxy Statement materials, we are: 1) mitigating greenhouse gas emissions in our operations,
including investments since 2000 of nearly $7 billion on emissions-reduction technology; 2) delivering solutions that help our customers reduce their own emissions and improve their energy efficiency; 3) supporting research that can change the way
we produce and use energy and assist society in transitioning to lower carbon energy solutions; and 4) participating in constructive dialogue on policy options for society to address the dual challenge of meeting the world&#146;s growing energy
needs while also addressing the risks posed by climate change. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><I></I></B><B>Carbon Risk Considerations Addressed in</B><B><I> Outlook for Energy
</I></B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The foundation for ExxonMobil&#146;s strategic decisions, business plans, and investments, is our <I>Outlook for Energy</I>
(<U>www.exxonmobil.com/energyoutlook)</U>. Updated annually to account for changes in global demand, supply, policy framework, and technology, the <I>Outlook</I> broadly aligns with third-party forecasts such as the International Energy Agency
(IEA). </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The <I>Outlook </I>reflects increasingly stringent climate policies and is generally consistent with the aggregated Nationally Determined
Contributions which were submitted by signatories to the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change 2015 Paris Agreement. Our <I>Outlook</I> seeks to identify potential impacts of climate related policies, which often target specific
sectors, by using various assumptions and tools including application of a proxy cost of carbon to estimate potential impacts on consumer demands. To enhance the robustness of our <I>Outlook</I>, we assess a wide range of assumptions for key supply
and demand drivers to test the range of potential outcomes. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>2&deg;C Scenario Addressed </B></P>

investment processes, detailed further in the ECS, appropriately contemplate and address climate-related risks and are sufficient to ensure delivery of long-term shareholder value. </P>
