<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">IRVING, Texas &#150; ExxonMobil today filed its <U>definitive proxy statement</U> and a letter to shareholders, urging them to vote using the BLUE CARD to
support the company&#146;s 12 director nominees at its 2021 annual meeting of shareholders on May 26. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;Our directors have experience leading some of
the world&#146;s largest, most complex and successful companies and bring to the board a wide range of backgrounds, knowledge and skills relevant to ExxonMobil&#146;s business and future success,&#148; said Darren Woods, chairman and chief executive
officer. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;Over the past several years, the board has added directors with deep expertise in climate change, financial markets, capital allocation,
energy transition, and environmental, social and governance practices. New directors also have significant experience helping companies navigate complex transitions while building value for shareholders.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">ExxonMobil&#146;s board of directors oversees the business plans, which through 2025 are expected to increase earnings and cash flow to fund and grow the
dividend, pay down debt and invest in future projects. The plan, which is flexible to market conditions and benefits from ongoing cost-reduction efforts, positions ExxonMobil to emerge from the pandemic with improved financial performance and win in
a lower-carbon energy future. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;We are investing in commercially attractive <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">low-carbon</FONT> technologies, which
will be an integral part of our long-term strategy,&#148; said Woods. &#147;We ask our shareholders to support our strong and diverse board to continue the disciplined focus on executing this plan that sustains the dividend and leverages our
expertise and competitive advantages.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">ExxonMobil&#146;s board has added seven independent, highly-qualified directors since 2016, including three
directors this year. The company&#146;s director nominees have an average tenure of approximately five years, compared to an average of nearly eight years for the boards of S&amp;P 500 companies. Eleven of the board&#146;s 12 director nominees are
