<P STYLE="margin-top:41pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:26.0pt; font-size:14pt; font-family:arial">As we approach ExxonMobil&#146;s annual shareholders meeting later this month, I wanted to highlight two important issues to the company &#150;
climate change and shareholder engagement. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:26.0pt; font-size:14pt; font-family:arial">We feel this is important because two funds representing retirees and other ExxonMobil investors,
the Church of England and New York State Common Retirement Fund, are currently misrepresenting our position on these important matters in an effort to influence voting at our annual shareholders meeting later this month. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:41pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:26.0pt; font-size:14pt; font-family:arial"><B>Managing Climate Change Risks </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:26.0pt; font-size:14pt; font-family:arial">ExxonMobil is committed to doing its part to address the dual challenge of meeting the world&#146;s growing demand for energy while reducing the
risks of climate change. </P>

ExxonMobil is providing customers to help them reduce their emissions and improve energy efficiency. </P>

senior management to hear multiple perspectives, including conflicting points of view, and to incorporate those views as appropriate. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:41pt; margin-bottom:0pt; line-height:26.0pt; font-size:14pt; font-family:arial">In 2018,
