<Center><DIV STYLE="width:8.5in" align="left">
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>ARTICLE FOR PUBLICATION ON ENERGY FACTOR EUROPE </B></P>
<P STYLE="font-size:18pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">


<IMG SRC="g174878g0325184026524.jpg" ALT="LOGO">
 </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>TITLE: Parallel Research: Accelerating Innovation </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Collaboration is an essential part of good research. However many brilliant minds a single organization has, the way that knowledge is exchanged with other
experts is a key element of developing new ideas. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">But what does that mean in practice? Here&#146;s where the concept of &#147;parallel research&#148;
plays a pivotal role. To make sense of what parallel research is, we have to look at how scientific research works &#150; a process we call the &#145;innovation value chain&#146;. That&#146;s the path an idea follows to go from a concept in a lab to
a fruitful pilot project. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>The Innovation Value Chain </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Powerful ideas and progress can come from anywhere &#150; academia, <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">start-ups,</FONT> national laboratories, and established
companies. Each of these organizations occupy a niche role in innovation, bringing different skillsets and resources to the process. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Among these
organizations, academia has often been the place of genesis for novel concepts that can transform the energy industry. A university would normally conduct tests to establish &#145;proof of principle&#146;. That means they will attempt to show that
an idea for a new technology would work, in terms of fundamental science. Theoretical research in fuel cells at the University of Genoa in Italy, a project with the potential to accelerate carbon capture technologies for industrial facilities, is a
good example. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">With a proof of principle established, a national research institute &#150; such as, in the US, one of the national laboratories &#150;
might create a small-scale demonstration. Bringing in decades of expertise in process optimization and feasibility analysis, the national labs take the idea from a &#145;scientifically possible in theory&#146; scale, to a &#145;working in
reality&#146; scale. From micro, to macro. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">With promising findings from laboratory and pilot studies, novel technologies will require an initial
deployment at scale. Often, <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">start-up</FONT> companies specializing in that field of technology play a crucial role at this stage. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Ultimately, to bring a new technology to commercialization, a global energy company like ExxonMobil &#150; with the capabilities, capital and experience of
managing large projects around the world &#150; joins the innovation value chain and searches for pathways to scale up deployment. </P>
</DIV></Center>


<p style="margin-top:1em; margin-bottom:0em; page-break-before:always">
<HR SIZE="3" style="COLOR:#999999" WIDTH="100%" ALIGN="CENTER">


<Center><DIV STYLE="width:8.5in" align="left">
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">However, while this innovation value chain may sound like a linear process, when it comes to tackling
pressing challenges in energy and the environment, it is everything but. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#145;We don&#146;t have time to be linear&#146;, says Dr Vijay Swarup, vice
president of R&amp;D at ExxonMobil. &#145;How do you speed up this process? You do things in parallel.&#146; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Working in Parallel </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">ExxonMobil is doing what we can to speed up the research and development of new energy technologies by working in parallel. This means that we are instigating
research ourselves, bringing ideas to the lab and working alongside the institutions we need to bring them to fruition, researching fundamental science in parallel with the practical engineering. This enables us to collaborate to develop the ideas
which we can see have the greatest potential to be scaled up. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Vijay recently explained the benefits of parallel research in February at the IP Week
Conference in a session titled &#147;Innovation for the Energy Transition: Challenges and Opportunities for Research, Innovation and Development&#148; <I>(link to IP Week speech &#150; previously filed with SEC)</I> </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">To advance emerging energy technologies, ExxonMobil is collaborating with over 80 universities around the world, five energy centres and U.S national
laboratories. Universities in Europe include the University of Genoa as well as Imperial College London. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">We have built many relationships with smaller
specialist firms to deploy pilot demonstrations. As an example, when we wanted to develop a direct air carbon capture system using a materials technology, <U>we engaged with Mosaic Materials</U>. But to develop an
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">engineering-led</FONT> carbon capture and storage (CCS) technology <U>we are working with Global Thermostat</U>. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The
ExxonMobil R&amp;D team are working on many new technologies, as we search for solutions to the dual challenge of how to provide the energy people need whilst managing environmental impacts and the risks of climate change. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Collaborating in parallel means that every step of the way, we are exchanging knowledge with external experts &#150; and that we can make progress faster on
this critical endeavour. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center">- ends - </P>
