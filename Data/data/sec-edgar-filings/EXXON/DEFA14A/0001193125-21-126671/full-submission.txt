 </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Tze San Koh, Presdient, China Gas Marketing, recently spoke at IP week. Below is a summary of her comments. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The impact of <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Covid-19</FONT> on the Asian Natural Gas market </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">China is the largest energy consumer in the world, displaying a massive energy demand that is projected to to grow as the Chinese government focuses on
bringing more of its citizens out of poverty and more towards the middle class. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">China is one of the few countries that had positive GDP growth in 2020 at
2.3%<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">1.</SUP> Despite the pandemic and economic slowdown, China&#146;s Natural Gas demand for the year increased due to its environmental competitiveness vs. coal<SUP
STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">2</SUP>. China&#146;s LNG imports in 2020 increased by more than 10% to 67MTA vs 2019. LNG growth is higher than overall average Natural Gas growth&#151;thanks to LNG&#146;s cost competiveness over other
Natural Gas sources (e.g., domestic gas, imported pipeline gas) especially in coastal regions. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">China&#146;s share of Natural Gas in its primary energy
mix is only 8.3%, much lower than the global average of 24.8%, signifying growth potential. Recently, the World Bank projected China&#146;s 2021 GDP growth to reach 7.9%. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">All signs indicate China&#146;s Natural Gas and LNG market has recovered from <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19,</FONT> and will continue to grow in
the future. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The short-term carbon neutral vision for the Asian Natural Gas markets In 2020, Chinese President Xi announced during the UN General Assembly
that China aims to have CO2 emissions peak before 2030 and to achieve carbon neutrality before 2060. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Coal accounted for about 58% of China&#146;s total
energy consumption and 66% of its electricity generation. <SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">3</SUP> </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Natural Gas can provide improved air
quality and reduced greenhouse gases when compared to coal in a range of applications. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The use of natural gas in power generation plays an important role
in reducing global emissions. When considering life cycle emissions, natural gas emits up to 60&nbsp;percent lower greenhouse gases and produces significantly fewer air pollutants than coal for power generation. Natural gas also provides a reliable
source of power to supplement renewable energy when wind or solar power is not available. <B></B> </P>

<Center><DIV STYLE="width:8.5in" align="left">
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">LNG is anticipated to remain cost competitive due to abundant natural gas resources and an increase in
aspiring exporters. As a result, cost competitive LNG supply relative to other gas sources should have an advantagin the marketplace. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The steady progress
of Natural Gas market reform in China has led to the continuous and rapid development of the Natural Gas market in the past decade. Significant progress has been made in the relaxation of price regulation, the establishment of Natural Gas trading
centers, the promotion of third-party access to Natural Gas infrastructure, and the establishment of PipeChina at the end of 2020. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">This combination of
demand growth, supply abundance and greater accessibility means that natural gas is making an important contribution to enhancing energy security which is an important consideration in China&#146;s energy mix in the next decade. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The Carbon Neutral Vision for the Asian Natral Gas markets beyond 2035 China natural gas demand in the next decade is expected to grow. In the near term,
during the 14th five-year plan, switching to Natural Gas from coal or oil products would immediately reduce emissions. In the medium-term, through the continued development and deployment of <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">low-cost</FONT> and
highly efficient Natural Gas technologies, natural gas also provides a reliable source of power to supplement renewable energy when wind or solar power is not available. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Progressively over the longer term, <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">low-carbon</FONT> technologies&#150;including carbon capture, utilization, and storage
(CCUS) can provide an efficient and cost effective pathway to reduce GHG emissions. These technologies are particularly relevant for sectors where emissions are difficult or very costly to abate through other means. They can also capitalize on the
use of existing natural gas infrastructure to minimize capital investment.<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">4</SUP> </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Aligned with such,
ExxonMobil has created a new business to commercialize its extensive <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">low-carbon</FONT> technology portfolio. The new business, ExxonMobil Low Carbon Solutions, will initially focus on carbon capture and storage, one
of the critical technologies that can help society to achieve the climate goals outlined in the Paris Agreement. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">ExxonMobil has more than 30 years of
experience in CCS technology and was the first company to capture more than 120&nbsp;million tonnes of CO2, which is equivalent to the emissions of more than 25&nbsp;million cars for one year. ExxonMobil has an equity share in about <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">one-fifth</FONT> of global CO2 capture capacity and has captured approximately 40&nbsp;percent of all the captured anthropogenic CO2 in the world. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">With our demonstrated leadership in carbon capture and emissions reduction technologies, ExxonMobil is committed to meeting the demand for affordable energy
while reducing emissions and managing the risk of climate change. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">To learn more about ExxonMobil&#146;s low carbon solutions please visit </P>
