<P STYLE="margin-top:24pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Bank of America Announces 2030 Financing Activity Targets as Part of Net Zero Commitment </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>Committed to achieve net zero in its financing activities, operations and supply chain before 2050 </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Charlotte, N.C. &#151; Bank of America today announced 2030 targets for reducing emissions associated with financing activities related to three key sectors:
auto manufacturing, energy and power generation. The targets are outlined in the company&#146;s Approach to Zero<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&#153;</SUP>,<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">1</SUP> which details Bank of
America&#146;s net zero greenhouse gas emissions approach and target setting process. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;This work goes hand in hand with the way we are engaging our
clients &#150; those that have set their own net zero goals, those working toward future goals, and those that are in the supply chain of companies that have a goal &#150; to help ensure a just, stable transition to the sustainable future we all
want,&#148; said Brian Moynihan, Chief Executive Officer at Bank of America. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Setting 2030 Targets for Financing Activities </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">To develop emission reduction targets related to its financing activity, Bank of America drew from its understanding of climate science and the guidance
provided by industry collaborations in which the company participates including the <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Net-Zero</FONT> Banking Alliance and the Sustainable Markets Initiative. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Bank of America&#146;s targets initially cover emissions related to its committed credit exposure to clients in auto manufacturing, energy and power
generation. For each of these sectors, the company has set targets based on emissions intensity, calculated on a weighted average for each portfolio of clients: </P> <P STYLE="font-size:6pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>

<Center><DIV STYLE="width:8.5in" align="left">
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Bank of America plans to set targets for other key sectors through 2024 and update targets to include
capital markets activities when the methodology is finalized and released by Partnership for Carbon Accounting Financials (PCAF). As data continues to improve and the science evolves, the company plans to review its targets to ensure they remain
relevant and in alignment with its net zero before 2050 goal. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Bank of America plans to disclose its 2019 financed emissions baselines for the auto
manufacturing, energy and power generation sectors, along with 2020 data, later this year in its Task Force on Climate-related Disclosures (TCFD) report.<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">2</SUP> The company also plans to disclose the
financed emissions for its entire business loan portfolio in 2023. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Bank of America met its carbon neutrality and 100% renewable electricity goals for its
operations in 2019, a year ahead of schedule. Since then, the company has set 2030 operations and supply chain goals,<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">3</SUP> including a commitment<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">4</SUP>
to utilize sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) for at least 20% of the company&#146;s total annual corporate and commercial jet fuel usage. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In April 2021,
the company announced a $1 trillion by 2030 goal<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">5</SUP> to mobilize capital to accelerate the environmental transition, this as part of its $1.5 trillion sustainable finance commitment to support the
United Nations Sustainable Development Goals. In 2021, Bank of America mobilized and deployed approximately $250&nbsp;billion in sustainable finance activity of which approximately $155&nbsp;billion was for climate and environmental transition.
Since 2007, Bank of America has mobilized more than $350&nbsp;billion toward climate and environment-related business activities. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Additional
environmental, social and governance (ESG) metrics, progress, and disclosures can be found in the company&#146;s Annual Report to Shareholders,<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">6</SUP> ESG Performance document,<SUP
STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">7</SUP> Environmental and Social Risk Policy Framework<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">8</SUP> and Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures<SUP
STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">2</SUP>. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Visit www.bankofamerica.com/environment to learn more about Bank of America&#146;s environmental
